## Session 2 — Hero video mobile autoplay & scroll glitch

### What we worked on
Making the landing-page hero video reliably autoplay on mobile with no iOS play button ever showing, plus fixing a scroll glitch.

### Completed
- Hero video now plays on mobile with no play button: video stays transparent (`opacity:0`) until playback genuinely starts, then reveals via an `is-playing` class added by JS in `index.html`.
- Added a poster still-frame (`images/hero-poster.jpg`, extracted from the first frame of `hero-video.mp4`) shown behind the video so iOS Low Power Mode shows a clean still instead of a play button.
- Force-play script: retries `.play()` on load, `canplay`, `pause`, visibility change, and resumes on first user touch/scroll/click (Low Power Mode requires a gesture).
- Fixed scroll glitch — parallax in `js/main.js` now drives both the video and the poster together (via `requestAnimationFrame`), and the 8s transform transition that caused the slow "drift back up" was removed.
- Committed and pushed to `origin/main`; Netlify auto-deploys to https://retrocloset.org.

### Next steps
- Confirm the live deploy at https://retrocloset.org looks correct on a real phone (with Low Power Mode on to verify the no-play-button behavior).

### Open questions & blockers
- None.

### Key decisions made
- Decision: Hide the iOS play button by keeping the `<video>` transparent until playback actually starts, rather than via CSS.
  Why: Modern iOS makes the centered "start playback" button un-hideable through `-webkit-media-controls` CSS; since the button lives on the video element, a transparent video hides the button too. A poster still-frame sits behind it for the Low Power Mode case.
- Decision: Drive the parallax on both the video and the poster together and remove the 8s transform transition.
  Why: Parallax previously moved only the video, so the poster's top edge peeked out; the 8s transform easing made the layer "slowly drift back up" on fast scroll. Moving both layers in sync with no transform transition eliminates both artifacts.

## Session 3 — Mobile touch-to-flip product cards

### What we worked on
Added a mobile touch-to-flip interaction for product cards so phone users can see the jersey back.

### Completed
- Gated the desktop hover-flip behind `@media (hover: hover)` in `css/styles.css` to stop sticky-hover flips on touchscreens, and added a `.product-card__flip.is-flipped` rule reusing the existing 0.7s transition.
- Added delegated, passive `touchstart`/`touchmove`/`touchend`/`touchcancel` handlers in `js/main.js` that flip the single card under the finger via `document.elementFromPoint`; never call `preventDefault`, so vertical scroll is preserved.
- Verified locally with `netlify dev` (port 8888): `/api/get-products` returns 37 products with front + back images.
- Committed (`382afea`) and pushed to `origin/main`, triggering the Netlify auto-deploy.

### Next steps
- Confirm the Netlify production deploy went green in the dashboard.
- Real-device check: test touch & hold, drag-to-scroll flip-in-turn, and size-pill / Add-to-Cart taps on an actual phone (only DevTools touch emulation was used locally). Also verify the `index.html` "Featured" section.

### Open questions & blockers
- None.

### Key decisions made
- Decision: Gate the existing hover-flip behind `@media (hover: hover)`.
  Why: `:hover` is sticky on touch devices — a tap leaves the card flipped until you tap elsewhere; the media query scopes it to real pointer devices.
- Decision: Drive the mobile flip with delegated, passive touch handlers on `document` that never `preventDefault`.
  Why: Delegation handles cards rendered after the async product fetch (no per-card listeners); passive + no preventDefault keeps vertical scrolling fully intact; `elementFromPoint` on each move naturally flips each image the finger passes over while scrolling.
- Decision: Preview locally with `netlify dev` rather than a plain static server.
  Why: The catalogue/featured pages fetch `/api/get-products`, a Netlify function that pulls from Airtable using `AIRTABLE_API_KEY` from `.env`; a static server 404s that endpoint ("Couldn't load products right now / HTTP 404").
